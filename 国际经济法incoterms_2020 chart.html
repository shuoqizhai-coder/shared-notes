E Departure — Seller's minimum obligation
F Main Carriage Unpaid — Buyer arranges & pays for main transport
C Main Carriage Paid — Seller pays freight to destination, BUT risk passes at shipment
⚠ Critical Exam Point: In all C-group terms, the risk split and transport cost split occur at different points. Seller pays freight to the destination, but risk transfers to buyer at the port/carrier at shipment. If goods are damaged in transit, buyer bears the loss even though seller paid the freight.
D Arrival — Seller bears all risk & cost to destination
How to read this chart:
Each row = one Incoterm term. Three bars per row: Risk Transfer, Transport Costs, Insurance.
• Blue = seller's responsibility. Orange = buyer's responsibility.
• A red dashed divider means the risk and transport bars split at different points — this only happens in C-group, and it's the #1 exam trap.
• A solid green bar means the seller is obligated to buy insurance under that Incoterm (CIF and CIP only).
• For all other terms, the party bearing the risk typically arranges insurance, but it's not mandated by the Incoterm itself.
• DAP vs. DPU: the only difference is who unloads at destination. DPU = seller unloads; DAP = buyer unloads.
• DDP = DAP + seller handles import clearance & pays all duties/taxes.
